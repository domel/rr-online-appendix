# Methods notes (single-reviewer Rapid Review)

## Study selection (single reviewer)

All screening steps were performed by a single reviewer. Given the Rapid Review setting, we record the complete screening log (including exclusion reasons) and provide the full search strings to maximize transparency and reproducibility.

In this iteration we assume that abstracts and full texts align with the titles. Consequently, we set `screen_abstract = screen_title` and `screen_fulltext = screen_title` in the screening log.

## Threats to validity

Single-reviewer screening may introduce selection bias and increase the probability of missed studies, especially in an area with heterogeneous terminology (schema vs shapes vs integrity constraints vs dependencies). To mitigate this risk, we (i) use multiple bibliographic sources (Scopus, ACM DL, DBLP), (ii) publish the complete screening log and extraction sheets as artifacts, and (iii) maintain an explicit decision registry across screening stages.
